# QD (Quick Disconnect) Bushing — Complete Technical Reference

## Overview

QD bushings are split-taper locking bushings used to mount sprockets, sheaves (pulleys), and other components onto shafts. They use a tapered interference fit with cap screws that either pull the bushing into the hub (tightening) or push it out (removal). The system is standardized across all major manufacturers (Dodge/Baldor, TB Woods, Martin, Browning, Gates, etc.) — bushings and hubs are interchangeable between brands.

---

## 1. Standard Taper Specification

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Taper rate** | 0.500 inch per foot (on diameter) |
| **Half-angle** | ~1.193° |
| **Full included angle** | ~2.386° |
| **Taper ratio** | 1:24 (diameter) |

The taper is self-holding — friction prevents the bushing from loosening under normal operation. All QD series use this same taper.

### Selection Guidance
- **Multiple series available?** Choose the smallest series that meets your hub requirements for the most compact installation. Or choose a larger series for higher torque capacity.
- **Rule of thumb:** For a given shaft size, the bushing bore wall thickness must be sufficient — this is why larger shafts need larger series.

## 5. Weld-On QD Hubs

### What They Are

A **weld-on QD hub** (also called a "bushing-bore hub" or "QD weld-on hub") is a plain steel hub with a QD-tapered bore — **no teeth, no grooves, no sheave profile** — just a cylindrical or flanged steel body designed to be **welded onto a custom fabrication**.

### How They Differ from Standard QD Sprockets/Pulleys

| Feature | Standard QD Sprocket/Pulley | Weld-On QD Hub |
|---------|---------------------------|----------------|
| **Function** | Complete, ready-to-use sprocket or pulley | Blank hub for custom welding |
| **Teeth/Grooves** | Has sprocket teeth or belt grooves | None — plain cylindrical body |
| **Material** | Cast iron or steel | Typically steel (weldable) |
| **Usage** | Mount directly to shaft, drive chain/belt | Weld to plates, drums, wheels, etc. |
| **Customization** | Fixed geometry | User defines the driven component |

### Why Use Weld-On QD Hubs

1. **Custom sprockets/pulleys**: Weld onto a custom-machined or fabricated component to create a non-standard sprocket, wheel, or hub assembly that still benefits from QD shaft mounting.

2. **Wheel hubs**: Weld onto a wheel, drum, roller, or flywheel to give it a QD-style removable shaft mount. Very common on conveyor rollers, agricultural equipment, and industrial vehicles.

3. **Track drive sprockets**: For applications like the LifeTrac where a custom drive sprocket is needed — weld the QD hub into the sprocket center, and the shaft connection is QD-removable.

4. **Axle/shaft mounting**: Any time you need to attach a fabricated component to a shaft with easy removal capability.

5. **Fieldrepairability**: QD bushings can be removed and replaced without moving the driven equipment — critical for field service on construction/agricultural equipment.

## 7. Installation / Removal

### Installation (Standard — Bushing Seats Inside Hub)
1. Clean all mating surfaces (bushing OD taper, hub bore taper)
2. Slide bushing into hub with holes aligned
3. Place assembly on shaft with key installed
4. Insert cap screws into **tightening** holes (threaded in hub flange)
5. Alternately tighten cap screws to rated torque
6. Check alignment

### Removal
1. Remove cap screws from tightening holes
2. Insert one or more cap screws into the **removal** (jacking) holes — threaded in bushing flange
3. Tighten jacking screws to break taper and push bushing out of hub
4. Slide assembly off shaft

## 10. LifeTrac Application Notes

For the LifeTrac v25 track drive system, recommended QD bushing selections based on typical shaft sizes:

| Application | Typical Shaft | Recommended QD Series | Notes |
|-------------|--------------|----------------------|-------|
| Track drive sprocket | 1-1/2" – 2" | SD, SK, SF, or E | Weld-on hub welded to sprocket |

| Idler shaft | 3/4" – 1" | SH or SDS | Light-duty, easy replacement |
| PTO shaft | 1-3/8" | SD or SK | Standard PTO size |

### Why QD for LifeTrac:
- **Field serviceability**: Bushings can be swapped without pulling shafts or disassembling frames
- **Alignment flexibility**: Taper fit self-centers and grips without set screws marring the shaft
- **Standardization**: Available worldwide, multiple source suppliers, no proprietary parts
- **Weld-on hubs**: Allow using QD mounting on custom-fabricated sprockets for the track drive
